          Guide

          This is the in-app book for the current systems.

          Map controls

          Drag to pan, mouse wheel to zoom, and right click land to add villages/capitals or generate a frozen zoom popup. Heightmap mode shows elevation. Satellite/biome mode shows the generated biome layer.

          Settlements

          Create villages/capitals by right clicking the map. Link them to an empire from the Empires menu. Each settlement has its own supplies, infrastructure, population, buildings, fortifications and local army management.

          Empires

          Empires store wealth, total population and their linked settlements. Wealth changes every pass time from taxes, building income, soldier wages, building upkeep, and the manual wealth change modifier.

          Supplies

          Food and materials are settlement-level. Crops increase food; mines increase materials. Biome affects production. Population and raised armies consume food. Buildings and fortifications can consume materials or wealth.

          Infrastructure

          Infrastructure is settlement-level and admin-set. It does not change automatically. Each infrastructure point multiplies population growth by 1.2×.

          Population

          Population is settlement-level, but total population is shown at empire level. Growth depends on base growth, infrastructure, custom building modifiers and food availability. Food shortage slows growth or causes decline.

          Buildings

          Default buildings are crops, mines, markets and barracks. Crops improve food, mines improve materials, markets improve wealth gain, and barracks reduce soldier wages. Custom buildings can be created with up to three positive or negative modifiers.

          Fortifications

          Fortifications are settlement-level and have levels 1 to 5. Higher levels cost much more and require more material upkeep per pass time.

          Armies

          Armies contain many unit types. Each unit has a score value, hiring price, wage, food use and movement speed. Better units usually cost more, receive higher score, consume more food and demand higher wages. Their score changes according to strategy, terrain and biome. Armies can be disbanded; their wage is still paid once on the next pass time.

          Army movement speed is calculated from the average speed of all troops in the army, then reduced by slope, altitude and water crossing. Sea/water movement is slower.

          Terrain and biome are now intentionally decisive. Cavalry is heavily favored on open plains and lowlands, but severely punished in forests, marshes, cliffs and steep mountain terrain. Light footmen, skirmishers and berserker-style troops do better in forests, scrub and broken terrain. Archers like clear lines of sight, hills and open ground, but common archers suffer in dense forest. Pikemen and hoplites prefer open or defensive formation ground and dislike rough, wet, or steep terrain.

          Friendly ground matters. Armies within 5 km of their own village receive a small defensive boost, near their capital a stronger boost, and near their fortress the strongest defensive boost.

          Battles no longer simply wait for the timer to expire. They continue until a side reaches its break point, usually when only about 10–25% of the original force remains active after deaths and injuries. Fiercer elite troops such as berserkers, Huscarls and Varangian Guards are less likely to flee early.

          • Cavalry — score 2.0, price 14, wage 0.08, food 0.065, speed 38 km/day. Counters archers, light troops and Kush Archers; weak to pikemen and hoplites.
          • Light Footmen — score 0.75, price 4, wage 0.02, food 0.028, speed 26 km/day. Cheap rough-terrain troops.

          • Heavy Infantry — score 1.0, price 9, wage 0.05, food 0.046, speed 21 km/day. Counters pikemen and light troops.

          • Hybrid Infantry — score 1.15, price 11, wage 0.050, food 0.044, speed 24 km/day. Flexible infantry; counters light footmen, archers and Numidian Cavalry.

          Harbors, ships and wind

          Harbors are a default settlement type and must be placed on land within 3 km of the sea. A linked harbor opens a ship-building menu where you can buy groups of Fishing Boats, Raider Ships, Longboats, Sailboats, Biremes, Merchant Ships, Triremes, Quadriremes, Quinqueremes, or Custom ships.

          Ship groups move like armies, can trigger encounters and battles, can fuse with allied ship groups, and can carry land troops. Ground armies cannot cross water by themselves; if ordered across water, they stop at the shore. Ships can only move on water.

          To transport troops, move a ship group within 5 km of a ground army and use Load army into ships. When the fleet reaches another shore, use Release troops to shore. Ship capacity is multiplied by the number of ships in the group.

          Wind / sea mode shows generated wind arrows over the sea. Ships move faster with the wind and slower against it. Sail-heavy vessels such as Fishing Boats and Sailboats are more affected by wind than oared ships like Biremes and Triremes. Refresh wind generates a new sea wind map.

          Diplomacy

          Diplomacy sets relations between two empires as Neutral, Enemies or Allies. This is stored now and can later decide whether armies fight automatically.

          Pass time

          Pass time advances settlement supplies, population, buildings, fortification upkeep, army wages and empire wealth. Buildings under construction reduce their remaining build time by one each pass.

          Export / Import

          Export saves the current campaign state as a JSON file. Import restores saved settlements, empires, armies, diplomacy, routes, economy, buildings and other campaign state.
